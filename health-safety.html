Health & Safety Policy

Our commitment to maintaining the highest standards of health and safety

Last Updated: February 2024

1. Policy Statement

Crisp Interiors is committed to ensuring the health, safety, and welfare of all our employees, contractors, clients, and members of the public who may be affected by our work activities. We recognize that effective health and safety management is essential to our business success.

2. Legal Compliance

We are committed to complying with all relevant health and safety legislation, including but not limited to:

  • Health and Safety at Work Act 1974
  • Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999
  • Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015
  • Control of Substances Hazardous to Health Regulations 2002
  • Work at Height Regulations 2005
  • Control of Asbestos Regulations 2012

3. Responsibilities

3.1 Management

  • Provide adequate resources for health and safety
  • Ensure risk assessments are carried out
  • Provide appropriate training and supervision
  • Monitor and review health and safety performance
  • Lead by example in health and safety matters

3.2 Employees

  • Take reasonable care for their own health and safety
  • Co-operate with the company on health and safety matters
  • Report any hazards, incidents, or near misses
  • Use equipment and PPE correctly
  • Attend required health and safety training

4. Key Health and Safety Practices

4.1 Risk Assessment

We conduct thorough risk assessments for all work activities. These are regularly reviewed and updated to ensure they remain current and effective.

4.2 Method Statements

Detailed method statements are prepared for all significant work activities, outlining safe working procedures and control measures.

4.3 Training

All employees receive appropriate health and safety training, including:

  • Induction training
  • Site-specific training
  • Task-specific training
  • Regular refresher training

4.4 Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Appropriate PPE is provided, maintained, and replaced as necessary. All employees are required to use PPE correctly and report any defects.

4.5 Working in Healthcare Environments

Given our specialization in healthcare interiors, we maintain additional protocols:

  • Infection control procedures
  • Working around patients and medical staff
  • Out-of-hours working to minimize disruption
  • Coordination with hospital health and safety teams
  • Emergency procedures specific to healthcare facilities

5. CHAS Accreditation

We are CHAS (Contractors Health and Safety Assessment Scheme) registered, demonstrating our compliance with health and safety legislation and best practices.

6. Incident Reporting

All accidents, incidents, and near misses are reported and investigated. We use these investigations to identify root causes and implement preventive measures.

7. Emergency Procedures

Clear emergency procedures are established for all work sites, including:

  • Fire evacuation procedures
  • First aid arrangements
  • Reporting lines and emergency contacts
  • Coordination with site emergency procedures

8. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually or following significant changes to legislation, work activities, or following incidents.
