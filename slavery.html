Modern Slavery Policy

Our commitment to preventing modern slavery and human trafficking

Last Updated: February 2024

1. Our Commitment

Crisp Interiors is committed to preventing modern slavery and human trafficking in all aspects of our business operations and supply chain. We have a zero-tolerance approach to modern slavery and we are committed to acting ethically and with integrity in all our business dealings and relationships.

2. What is Modern Slavery?

Modern slavery is a term used to encompass slavery, servitude, forced and compulsory labour, bonded and child labour and human trafficking. Victims are coerced, deceived and forced against their free will into providing work or services.

3. Our Business

Crisp Interiors provides specialist healthcare interior refurbishment services. We operate primarily in the United Kingdom, working with private hospitals and healthcare facilities. Our supply chain includes:

  • Building materials suppliers
  • Specialist contractors and subcontractors
  • Equipment and furniture suppliers
  • Professional service providers

4. Policies and Procedures

We have implemented the following measures to prevent modern slavery:

4.1 Supplier Due Diligence

  • Conduct risk assessments on all new suppliers
  • Require suppliers to confirm compliance with modern slavery legislation
  • Include modern slavery clauses in contracts
  • Regular supplier audits where appropriate

4.2 Employee Awareness

  • Provide training on recognizing signs of modern slavery
  • Clear reporting mechanisms for concerns
  • Regular policy updates and communication

4.3 Recruitment Practices

  • Robust recruitment processes including right-to-work checks
  • No recruitment fees charged to workers
  • Fair wages and working conditions for all employees
  • Transparent employment contracts

5. Risk Assessment

We have assessed our business and supply chain for modern slavery risks. While we consider our risk to be relatively low given our UK-based operations, we remain vigilant and continuously monitor:

  • Geographic risk factors in our supply chain
  • Sector-specific risks in construction and healthcare
  • High-risk products and materials
  • Use of temporary or agency workers

6. Due Diligence Processes

Our due diligence processes include:

  • Identifying and assessing potential risk areas in our supply chain
  • Mitigating the risk of slavery and human trafficking occurring in our supply chain
  • Monitoring potential risk areas in our supply chain
  • Protecting whistle blowers

7. Training

To ensure a high level of understanding of the risks of modern slavery and human trafficking in our supply chains and our business, we provide training to relevant members of staff. All directors and senior management have been briefed on the subject.

We are committed to continuously improving our practices to combat slavery and human trafficking. This policy is reviewed annually and updated as necessary to ensure it remains effective and compliant with current legislation.
